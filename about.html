Early Life

Barbara Quesk was born in California in 1984. She always had the fascination with the legal side of the world, and entered into law school at 19. Soon, she worked as a criminal defense lawyer, helping keep innocent people free. During her time as a lawyer, she ran for various local positons, including the school board and later the district attorney and mayor. She was able to keep consistently high approval ratings, and consistently make her city one of the best ranked places to live in California.

Congress

Barbara was elected to congress in 2002 with 65% of the vote. She has served her district throughout these 14 years with integrity and incredibly high approval ratings. She has passed numerous laws to better the lives of the people in her district, and has fought to protect all children and all trees. Barbara has forged alliances across the aisle, and will continue to do so.

Accomplishments

Barbara has passed a slew of legislation to protect every person in her district. In her first term alone, she introduceed 20+ pieces of legislation. Her most successful ones have been the Keep The Streets Safe act, as well asco-signing the Safer Pharmecuticals Act. Barbara has also established the joint committee of protecting rivers, which has been cited by a third party commission as protecting over 200+ species from nearing exctiction.
In her second term, Barbara passed the Fund Education and Nasa NOW! act, which dedicated more funding to schools not just in her district, but across the states. She also successfully assisted in peace negotiations between Glaggleland and the Enfosi's.
In Barbaras 3rd term, she is maintaining her dedication to serving her district, and ensuring we maintain equal rights. She has already begun to introduce and co-sponsor critical legislation, and work for the people.
